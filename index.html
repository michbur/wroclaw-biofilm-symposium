Venue: Faculty of Biotechnology, University of Wrocław

The Faculty of Biotechnology in University of Wrocław is the oldest faculty of biotechnology in Poland. It is composed of 13 departments and 2 laboratories. It employs about 100 people, including 56 researchers. The main topic of research focus of attention on biotechnology, biochemistry, bioinformatics, microbiology, immunology, virology, cell biology or plants physiology. His areas of special expertise include protein engineering, molecular photosynthesis mechanisms, liposome technologies, cancer growth mechanisms, agricultural biotechnology and DNA evolution. Moreover, the faculty cooperates with a wide range of scientific centres in Poland and abroad, and with companies of the biotechnology industry. In last years, the Faculty of Biotechnology was appreciated for his level of research by the Committee for Evaluation of Scientific Units and it received the prestigious category A + (2013) among only two biological faculties in Poland. Additionally, as a member of the consortium Wrocław Centre for Biotechnology it received the status KNOW (Leading National Research Centre) for the years 2014 – 2018, thereby gaining access to extensive funding. The achievements in research and fully equipped research laboratories provide a comprehensive range of resources, skills and experience designed to develop knowledge and understanding of both the practice and theory of biotechnology and related areas of study.
